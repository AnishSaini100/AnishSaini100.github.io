I successfully made my own app running on a Nokia 106!

We normally think of brick phones as being basic and boring, they can only call and text. They are primarily marketed towards lower income people or those who want a digital detox free of distractions.

And this was not written in some low level programming langauge I had to learn. Believe it or not, I wrote this in plain HTML! Alongside CSS and Javascript.

The Story - Late 2025

I discovered November last year that there are some 4G models of Nokia sold in some poorer countries in Asia (such as India) that have a very special app loaded in them which enable to access apps such as Youtube and News.

I did a some more digging and found the company, which is CloudMosa, who made the Cloud Phone app. I then went to their website and found their developers section which explained you to test your apps on its simulator. Apparently, this doesn't run locally on your phone. Instead, it's a low res live stream of a browser running on a powerful server in another country. A brick phone's processor is simply far too weak to render HTML and run javascript by itself.

I then registered on their website using my personal e-mail address and I was led to a dashboard with the simulator. It is simply an image of a brick phone with a preview attatched to its screen. It allows you to click its buttons like how you'd use it in real life. Running it led me to the exact same home page of the apps. Because it's browser based I can even securely sign in with my Gmail account.

You can add URLs of your own websites. Some websites are just not suited for such a compact screen so they look messy. To run it on a physical device you need to add the IMEI number of the phone. At that time I had the 2G version so it unfortunately didn't have this special app loaded in it. I needed the 4G version.

Summer 2026

Since it was too good I told my parents to upgrade my phone from 2G to 4G because 2G is from the 1990s and its likely to be phased out very soon. I gave them the exact model (Nokia 106 4G) I wanted and told them I wanted the Indian version specifically.

On my 15th Birthday I got it as a surprise gift from my parents. They had imported the Indian version and it wasn't too expensive. It was the moment of truth if this phone had the wonderful app. Luckily it did and I can't express how happy I was at that moment.

I went to the developer page again and I entered the IMEI number of my phone and it worked! I added the URL of the Amazon website and it appeared on my apps screen. It ran just as well on the phone as on the simulator.

I wrote some code and this, what you're viewing is my app. Since it only accepted URLs and not HTML files I had to host it on Github and use it's URL instead.
